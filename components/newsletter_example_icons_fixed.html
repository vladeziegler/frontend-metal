The new battleground in african finance

The African financial market is changing fast. For years, M-Pesa was the undisputed leader in mobile money. Yet its market share has recently dropped to 90.8%. New fintechs are entering the field, backed by serious capital. Wave recently raised $137 million to expand across the continent. At the same time, consolidation is happening, with firms like Lesaka Technologies acquiring digital banks like Bank Zero to merge fintech speed with banking scale. This sets the stage for a new fight for the customer, where technology, specifically AI, will determine the winners.

AI's real return on investment

In this competitive climate, AI is not a cost. It is a tool for growth. Studies show AI investments in banking can produce a 3.7 to 4.2x return. This value comes from very practical applications. AI helps banks move beyond old credit scoring models and serve new customers. A University of Missouri study found that banks using AI could lend to remote borrowers with lower interest rates and fewer defaults. This technology directly addresses the challenge of financial inclusion, turning it into a real business opportunity.

From theory to practice: seizing the AI opportunity

The opportunity with AI is two-fold. First, it allows banks to reach new markets by using better data to assess risk. Second, it drives major efficiency gains by automating internal work. The path to adoption is becoming clearer. Between 2017 and 2019, AI use in US banks grew from 14% to 43%, showing a maturing industry. To succeed, however, leaders must address foundational challenges first. A careful, phased approach is essential.
  • Start with low-risk internal projects to build skills. Use AI to modernize code or improve back-office tasks first.
  • Prepare for a major shift in your workforce. Focus on retraining people and creating new, specialized technical roles.
  • Build a specific risk plan for AI. Your current third-party risk management is not enough for the unique threats AI presents.
Getting these fundamentals right is the necessary first step before scaling AI across your bank.

Your next move: lead with AI or get left behind

The choice for banking leaders is simple: embed AI into your strategy or risk becoming a commodity. This is not about technology for its own sake. It is about using AI to create a clear competitive edge. As we've seen with Lesaka's 'transformative' acquisition of Bank Zero, the goal is to serve customers better with modern tools. As we discussed on our podcast, this means a deliberate commitment. You start small, prove the value, and then scale. But you canâ€™t run advanced AI on old, fragmented technology. To truly use AI for personalized service and efficient operations, you need a unified platform underneath it all. This approach lets you deliver the digital convenience customers want, while preserving the human trust that secures their loyalty.

The competitive map in African banking is being redrawn by technology. AI has a proven business case, but realizing its value requires a disciplined, crawl-walk-run approach. Start internally, build your skills, and manage the specific risks before you scale.

AI unlocks profitable lending in untapped markets: A University of Missouri study reveals that banks leveraging AI can more accurately identify creditworthy small business borrowers in distant locations, offering them lower interest rates with fewer defaults. This finding suggests that AI is a powerful tool for overcoming the 'banking deserts' left by widespread branch closures, transforming remote lending from a high-risk venture into a scalable growth opportunity by analyzing dynamic, real-time data beyond traditional credit metrics. For leaders at large banks, this is a clear directive: investing in AI-driven underwriting is no longer just for efficiency, but a strategic imperative for tapping into new, underserved markets safely, growing loan portfolios, and meeting community reinvestment goals without the expense of physical expansion.
